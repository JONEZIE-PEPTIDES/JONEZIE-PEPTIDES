Storage

Storage and cold-chain basics

  • Store unopened lyophilized vials in a cool, dry, light-protected environment.
  • After reconstitution, refrigerate promptly and minimize repeated warm-to-cold cycles.
  • Label reconstitution dates and keep lot-level handling notes when comparing products.

Reconstitution

Concentration reference

  • Concentration = compound amount (mg) divided by dilution volume (mL).
  • Example: 10mg with 2mL dilution equals 5mg/mL.
  • Use Research Tools when you want a cleaner concentration read or compound-specific storage notes.

Preparation

Vial handling reminders

  • Confirm the vial label, strength, and target volume before handling begins.
  • Add diluent slowly along the vial wall to reduce agitation.
  • Gently rotate or swirl until dissolved, then refrigerate and record the mix date.
